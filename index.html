Intercontinental

2013-2018

In 2013, a search for meaning incited a cosmic series of events that brought me all around the world.

By responding to humanitarian crises and filling staffing gaps where needed, I learned how to quickly assimilate programmatic and operational tasks, coordinate events and reporting in multiple languages, and adapt to diverse cultures and teams. These functions helped shape me personally as well: to be decisive and flexible, collaborative yet autonomous, as well as pragmatic but creative.

Berlin

2018 - present

Between every job I had in the non-profit sector, I mulled over three choices: take another job, take a vacation, or go back to school. The thrill of travel and gainful employment often beat out my interests in higher education, but eventually living out of a suitcase lost its appeal and I felt it was time to learn more about what laid at the nexus of business, technology, and psychology.

This curiosity led me to the HMKW University of Applied Sciences for Media, Communication and Management to pursue a Masters in Business Psychology. The program seeded a deep interest in the forecasted effects of automation, particularly around the increased need of cross-functional teams in businesses and related moderators of performance. Moreover, these studies have allowed me to explore new domains of research as a working student.
